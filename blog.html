Iceland

Iceland certainly is a very unique location. It is defined by waterfalls, glaciers and volcanoes, and overal rough landscapes. Especially in winter, when strong winds are cutting through your face, leaving your body behind with a numb feeling, yet emotionally charged by the wonderful scenes infront of your eyes. At times, it feels like you are at the edge of the world, far away from everything and everyone.
If you're lucky and weather conditions/season allows, you will be greeted by the majestic Aurora Borealis. While I didn't have that luck this time around, I was still very much amazed by the sheer raw beauty Iceland has to offer. It was snowing a lot, with freezing temperatures, which added something very special to the already spectacular landscapes, shaped by the impeccable forces of mother nature.
Walking around with my camera, photographing the stunning scenes in front of my eyes, while being trashed by strong, icy winds and being surrounded by giant mountain ranges and glaciers made me feel extremely small. At the same time, I gained a strong sense of sharpness and energy, and I felt my mind was as clear as ever.
Those are the moments I live for, something I don't experience during my day to day life working my regular job in a bigger city.

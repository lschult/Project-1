Iceland

I have always been drawn to cold places. Perhaps it is the unique atmophere which is created by dark and gloomy landscapes, or perhaps it is the cold air I breathe, which makes me feel sharp and on the edge at all times.
I visited Iceland in late winter, and was immersed by its vast spectrum of mountains, waterfalls and natural formations. There is so much to see, and everything thrives by its uniquness and raw beauty.

I found this quote quite fitting:
“The problem with driving around Iceland is that you’re basically confronted by a new soul-enriching, breath-taking, life-affirming natural sight every five goddam minutes. It’s totally exhausting.”

Norway

Norway has to be one of the most visually stunning locations with landscpaes boasting a wide range of spectacular characteristics. I had the pleasure to visit this place, and I was hoping for the right conditions to encounter the majestic, beautiful aurora borealis. I should not be dissapointed, we had clear skies for most of the trip and I was immersed by intense displays of colour bands, dancing and wiggling along the nihght skies. Surely, a spectacle I will never forget.
